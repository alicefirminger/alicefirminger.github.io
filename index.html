Fall Out Boy

An American rock band formed in Chicago, in 2001. The band consists of lead vocalist and guitarist Patrick Stump, bassist Pete Wentz, drummer Andy Hurley, and guitarist Joe Trohman.

Fall Out Boy: An Album Timeline

  • May 6, 2003 - Fall Out Boy release their debut album, Take This To Your Grave.The album produced three singles, including the minor success Grand Theft Autumn/Where Is Your Boy.
  • May 3, 2005 - Fall Out Boy release their breakthrough album, From Under the Cork Tree. Spearheaded by the lead single Sugar, We're Goin Down, the album debuted at No. 9 on the US Billboard 200 with 168,000 first week sales, a position it stayed at for two non-consecutive weeks, earning the band their first top 10 album and becoming their longest charting and best-selling album.
  • February 6, 2007 - They release their third album Infinity on High, selling over 260,000 copies in its first week of sales and becoming the band's first number-one album.
  • December 10, 2008 - Folie à Deux (French for "A Madness Shared by Two") was the fourth studio album released by the band. The album's style moved away from early emo power chords and toward a wider variation in genres.
  • April 12, 2013 - The band release their fifth studio album Save Rock and Roll after a 4 year haitus. It debuted at number one on the U.S. Billboard 200 with 154,000 first week sales, earning the band its second career number one.
  • January 16, 2015 - American Beauty/American Psycho (sometimes abbreviated as AB/AP) was the band's sixth studio album. The album's release was preceded by the 4× Platinum top 10 lead single Centuries.
  • January 19, 2018 - Their seventh studio album Mania was released. It received largely mixed reviews from music critics, but fared well commercially, debuting at number-one on the US Billboard 200. However, it is the band's first album since their debut studio album to not produce a Billboard Hot 100 single on the chart. The album was nominated for the category of Best Rock Album at the 61st Annual Grammy Awards.
